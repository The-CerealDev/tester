About / sources & simplifications
  • Ring geometry, element order and K-values are taken from Dev/Lattice_Files/00_Simplified_Lattice/ISIS.{elements,sequence,strength} — real ISIS RCS values, 163.36282 m circumference, 10 superperiods.
  • Physics constants and conversion formulas are ported verbatim from Dev/01_Cycle_Time/cycle_time.py and Dev/02_Machine_State/{tune_control,correctors,machine_state,machine_state_defaults}.py.
  • The one-turn transfer-matrix / Twiss engine, closed-orbit Green's function, and betatron-wobble animation are this simulation's own equivalent of what MAD-X/cpymad would report — the real repo has no portable pure-Python formula for "actual tune" or orbit correction (both are MAD-X-only), so "Qx/Qy (simulated)" will not exactly match the Di-Wright-requested tune. That mismatch is physically realistic — it mirrors the real system.
  • Each real quadrupole is modelled as its full real 9-slice chain (8 fringe segments + 1 core, each with its own real K-value and length from ISIS.strength) rather than one collapsed effective thick quad — collapsing the fringes into a single core-only lens made the horizontal one-turn matrix unconditionally unstable (trace in the millions), which silently froze "Qx/Qy (simulated)" at an integer for every setting. The full real chain is stable.
  • At the default zero-trim ("bare") setting the vertical beta function swings to roughly 100 m at some QD/QDS locations — verified self-consistent (the Courant-Snyder invariant β·γ−α²=1 holds to ~1e-12 everywhere) rather than a numerical bug, but it reflects an unmatched bare lattice: real operation would additionally re-tune the main QD/QF/QDS family gradients (MAD-X's MATCH), not just the weak trim quads, to flatten this — that matching procedure isn't implemented here.
  • The RF cavity marker follows the PDF's single-period schematic (page 27) but does not exist in ISIS.elements/ISIS.sequence — schematic only.
  • The superperiod-9 sextupole is real (k2=0 in source) but shown as a static visual element — no portable nonlinear formula exists to animate it.
  • Aperture margin uses an illustrative ±40 mm placeholder half-width, not a repo-sourced value (real aperture data lives in an external CSV, not a portable default).
  • Element colours follow the PDF's beta-function-plot legend (pages 28/32/33): Dipole=olive, Defocus quad=red, Focus quad=blue, Sextupole=teal, Kicker=cyan, Marker=grey — chosen over the conflicting page-27 single-period cartoon since the legend is tied to the quantitative plots.
  • Harmonic sliders (D7SIN…F9COS) are shown because they are real MachineState fields, but no formula in the ported Python connects them to tune/orbit/envelope — they are inert here.
  • Click any coloured element in the 3D view to fly the camera to it and see its details, or use the "Focus" dropdown in the top bar to jump straight to a superperiod.
